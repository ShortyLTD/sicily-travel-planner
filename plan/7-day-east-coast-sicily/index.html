7-Day East Coast Sicily Itinerary: Volcanoes, Ruins & Baroque Towns

The east coast is Sicily's most popular route for first-timers — and for good reason. In 7 days you'll see a live volcano, a Greek clifftop theatre, and one of Europe's oldest cities, all connected by a single coastal road.

7 nights
Best for: First-timers, history lovers, couples
Catania → Mount Etna → Taormina → Syracuse → Ortigia
Recommended airport: Catania (CTA)

Your day-by-day plan

ARRIVAL

Day 1: Arrive Catania & Settle In

Arrive at Catania Fontanarossa airport. Collect your rental car or arrange a transfer to the city center. Spend the evening walking Via Etnea, the main pedestrian boulevard that points straight at Etna. Street food dinner: arancini, panelle, and croquettes from the vendors near the Duomo.

Tip: Most Catania hotels offer airport shuttle. If arriving after 8pm, skip the rental car pickup until morning — Catania's one-way streets are brutal in the dark.

Day 2: Mount Etna Day Trip

Drive to Rifugio Sapienza at 1,910 meters. Take the cable car and 4x4 jeep toward the summit craters. A guided crater hike with a volcanologist gives you the full story — lava flows, fumaroles, 360-degree views of the island. Return to Catania for dinner.

Tip: Book a morning slot. Clouds roll in by early afternoon, especially in summer. Bring layers — it's 15-20 degrees cooler at the top.

TRAVEL DAY

Day 3: Transfer to Taormina

Drive or take the bus to Taormina (90 minutes from Catania). Check in, then head straight to the Greek Theatre — 3rd century BC, carved into the cliff with views of Etna and the Ionian Sea. It's the single most photographed spot in Sicily. Dinner in Piazza IX Aprile.

Tip: Parking in Taormina is limited and expensive. The Lumbi car park at the north end of town is the most reliable option. Consider ditching the car and taking the Interbus from Catania.

Day 4: Taormina — Corso Umberto & Castelmola

Walk Corso Umberto, the main pedestrian street lined with boutiques and cafes. In the afternoon, take a quick trip up to Castelmola — a tiny medieval village perched above Taormina with even better views. Try the local almond wine at Bar Turrisi. Return for a rooftop bar sunset and dinner.

Tip: Castelmola is a 20-minute drive or a steep but scenic 45-minute hike from Taormina. The walk down is easier than the walk up.

TRAVEL DAY

Day 5: Transfer to Syracuse & Ortigia

Drive south to Syracuse (2.5 hours). Check into accommodation on Ortigia island — the historic heart of the city, connected to the mainland by two short bridges. Walk the baroque streets: Syracuse Cathedral (built into a Greek temple), Piazza Duomo, Fonte Aretusa. Harborside seafood dinner.

Tip: Ortigia is small and mostly pedestrianized. Stay on the island itself for the best experience. Parking is available at the Talete garage near the bridge.

Day 6: Ortigia — Markets & Archaeology

Morning at Ortigia's fish market — fishmongers shouting, locals bargaining, swordfish laid out on marble slabs. In the afternoon, choose between a boat tour around Ortigia (sea caves, coastal views) or the Neapolis Archaeological Park on the mainland (Greek theatre, the Ear of Dionysius). Sunset at Ortigia's southern point.

Tip: The fish market is only open in the morning, and it's quieter (and less touristy) before 9am. Get there early for the real atmosphere.

DEPARTURE

Day 7: Return to Catania & Depart

Drive back to Catania (1.5 hours). If time allows, visit La Pescheria — Catania's famous fish market — for last-minute shopping and a final Sicilian meal. Drop off the rental car and head to the airport.

Tip: Allow 2 hours between your last market stop and your flight. Catania airport is 15 minutes from the city center but security queues can be slow in summer.

Frequently asked questions

Is 7 days enough for east coast Sicily?

Yes. 7 days is the sweet spot for the east coast. You can comfortably cover Catania, Mount Etna, Taormina, and Syracuse without rushing. If you had fewer than 5 days, you would need to skip a destination.

Do I need a car for east coast Sicily?

Helpful but not essential. Catania to Taormina and Taormina to Syracuse are well connected by Interbus. However, a car gives you flexibility for Etna and smaller stops. If you do rent, book early — summer demand is high.

What is the best time to visit east coast Sicily?

May, June, September, and October offer the best balance of warm weather and manageable crowds. July and August are hot and busy. April is pleasant but some beach services may not be open yet.
