5-Day West Coast Sicily Itinerary: Food, Villages & Salt Ponds

The west coast is where Sicily feels most like itself — less polished than the east, more raw, more delicious. In 5 days you'll eat the best street food in Italy, climb a medieval hilltop with views to Tunisia, and photograph pink salt ponds at golden hour.

5 nights
Best for: Food lovers, photographers, smaller groups
Palermo → Erice → Trapani → Cefalu
Recommended airport: Palermo (PMO)

ARRIVAL

Day 1: Arrive Palermo & Street Food

Arrive at Palermo airport. Transfer to the city center and check in. Head straight to Ballaro market for your first real Sicilian meal on the street: arancini (fried rice balls), panelle (chickpea fritters), spleen sandwich (pane ca meusa), and fresh pasta with sardines. This is not tourist food — it's how Palermitans actually eat.

Tip: Ballaro market winds down by 6pm. If you arrive late, try the Vucciria area instead — it transitions into a lively outdoor bar scene after dark.

Day 2: Palermo — Normans & Beaches

Morning at Palazzo dei Normanni and the Cappella Palatina — the most jaw-dropping Byzantine mosaics in the Mediterranean, all gold and blue on every surface. Afternoon: explore the Quattro Canti intersection and baroque streets, or take the bus to Mondello Beach (30 minutes) for a swim. Dinner in the historic center.

Tip: The Cappella Palatina opens at 8:15am. Get there at opening to have it almost to yourself before tour groups arrive at 10.

Day 3: Erice & Trapani Salt Flats

Drive west to Erice (1.5 hours from Palermo) — a medieval hilltop village with cobblestone streets, castle ruins, and views that stretch to Tunisia on clear days. Stop at Maria Grammatico's pastry shop for almond pastries. In the afternoon, drive down to the Trapani salt flats: geometric pink and white pools stretching to the horizon. Perfect for photography.

Tip: The salt flats are most photogenic in late afternoon when the light turns golden. Time your Erice visit for morning, salt flats for 4-6pm.

Day 4: Cefalu — Beach & Cathedral

Drive east to Cefalu (2 hours). This is the postcard Sicily shot: golden beach, Norman cathedral with Byzantine mosaics, and a massive rock (La Rocca) behind it all. Lunch at a beachfront trattoria. Afternoon split between the beach and a hike up La Rocca for panoramic sunset views. Harbor dinner: pasta con le sarde and grilled swordfish.

Tip: La Rocca hike takes 45 minutes up. Start by 5pm in summer for sunset at the top. Bring water — there's no shade.

DEPARTURE

Day 5: Return to Palermo & Depart

Drive back to Palermo (1 hour from Cefalu). Final stop at Vucciria market for souvenirs — dried tomatoes, pistachio cream, capers from Pantelleria. A last lunch, then to the airport.

Tip: If your flight is after 4pm, you have time for one more Palermo walk. The Kalsa neighborhood and the botanical garden are both worth an hour.

Frequently asked questions

Is 5 days enough for west coast Sicily?

Yes, 5 days covers the highlights comfortably. You will see Palermo, Erice, the Trapani salt flats, and Cefalu. To add the Valley of the Temples in Agrigento, you would need 7 days.

Do I need a car for west coast Sicily?

Strongly recommended. Erice, the salt flats, and Cefalu are awkward to reach by public transport. Rental cars in Palermo start around 25-35 euros per day.

When is the best time to visit west coast Sicily?

April through June and September through October. The salt flats are most spectacular in summer when evaporation turns the water pink. Avoid August if you dislike crowds.
