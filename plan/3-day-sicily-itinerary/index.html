3-Day Sicily Itinerary: Hit the Highlights

Three days is tight, but it works — if you pick one coast and commit. Base yourself in Palermo or Catania, do one big day trip, and spend your time eating and exploring rather than driving between cities.

Your day-by-day plan

ARRIVAL

Day 1: Arrive & Explore Your Base

Arrive at your airport (Catania for the east coast, Palermo for the west). Transfer to the city center and check in. Spend the evening walking the old town — both cities have atmospheric street food markets, baroque architecture, and outdoor dining. Let the first evening be about absorbing the pace of the place.

Tip: With only 3 days, skip the rental car. Both Catania and Palermo are walkable city bases with good day trip connections by bus.

EXPLORE

Day 2: The Big Day Trip

This is your one big excursion. From Catania: Mount Etna cable car and crater hike, guided by a volcanologist. From Palermo: Cefalu (2 hours by train — beach, cathedral, La Rocca hike) or Erice (1.5 hours by car — medieval hilltop village with views to Tunisia). Pick one and give it the full day.

Tip: Book a guided Etna tour rather than going solo — the guides know the active craters and the geology, and transport is included. For Cefalu, the direct train from Palermo is cheap and scenic.

DEPARTURE

Day 3: Markets & Departure

Spend the morning at the main market: Ballaro in Palermo (chaotic, authentic, the best street food in Italy) or La Pescheria in Catania (fish market, volcanic energy, swordfish on marble slabs). Browse food, crafts, souvenirs. Light lunch, then airport.

Tip: Markets close by early afternoon. Get there before 10am for the full experience. Both are within walking distance of the city center.

Frequently asked questions

Is 3 days enough for Sicily?

Three days is enough to see one city and do one day trip well. You will not see the whole island, but you will get a genuine taste. The key is picking one coast and staying put rather than trying to cram both into a long weekend.

Should I base in Catania or Palermo for 3 days?

Catania if you want Mount Etna, Taormina access, and a grittier city vibe. Palermo if you want the best street food in Italy, Norman architecture, and day trips to Cefalu or Erice. Both are excellent — it depends on your priorities.

Can I see Taormina in a 3-day Sicily trip?

Yes, as a day trip from Catania (90 minutes by bus). But you will be rushing. If Taormina is your main goal, consider basing there instead of Catania and doing Etna as the day trip.
